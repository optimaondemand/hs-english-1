🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
A single word can promise that everything is about to change. How do you make the sentence keep that promise?
The volta is not only a mood shift; it is a grammatical event. Today you will see how the four sonnets build their turns out of a pivot word plus a real structural change, learn to spot the false volta, and engineer a genuine turn for your own draft.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 THINK FIRST · NO WRONG ANSWER
Which single English word most powerfully signals "everything I just said is about to change"?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

A volta has two parts. The first is the pivot word: "Yet," "But," "And yet," "O, no!" It is a flag planted in the line, telling the reader the argument has reached its limit. The second part is the one students skip: the structural change. After the flag, the sentences themselves must move differently. In Sonnet 29, after eight lines of dense, self-circling complaint, "Yet" opens onto a short, light clause: "Haply I think on thee." The syntax loosens, the imagery lifts (the lark at break of day), and the speaker's gaze swings from inward to outward. The grammar enacts the turn. In Sonnet 116 the pivot comes early, at line 5, and the change is a change of register: conditional negation ("Love is not love which alters…") snaps into direct assertion ("it is an ever-fixed mark"). A pivot word with no structural change behind it is a false volta: the flag waves, but the army never moves.

ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
The pivot word is the flag; the change is the volta. Test: what can the speaker say after the pivot that was impossible to say before it? If the answer is "nothing," the turn is not real yet.
ON THE BOARD · ENGINEERING A TURN AT LINE 9
The octave has argued (lines 7–8 of a student draft):
The trophies tarnish and the banners fall;
the season ends, and no one keeps the score.
Candidate line 9 — a FALSE volta (flag, no turn):
Yet still the trophies tarnish on the shelf.
Candidate line 9 — a GENUINE volta (flag + new sight):
Yet what we practiced stays inside the hands.
Both lines fly the pivot flag. The false volta repeats line 7 with "Yet" in front of it. The genuine one says something the octave could not say: the gold underline marks the new knowledge (what time cannot take), and the syntax shifts from things decaying to something held. That is a turn.

Where the four sonnets turn

Sonnet Pivot Line What actually changes
Sonnet 18"But thy eternal summer…"9From summer's failures to the beloved's exemption; poetry enters as the agent of permanence.
Sonnet 29"Yet in these thoughts…"9The gaze swings from inward misery to the thought of the beloved; the syntax loosens and lifts.
Sonnet 116"O, no!"5Early, deliberate turn: legal negation snaps into direct assertion. Register, not just topic, changes.
Sonnet 130"And yet, by heaven…"13Late turn: the whole mocking catalog is conceded as irrelevant, and honest love outbids false praise.
Notice the spread: line 9 is the home position, but 116 turns early and 130 turns late, and both do it on purpose. Your volta may move too, if you can say why.
✎ YOUR TURN · DECIDE IN YOUR NOTEBOOK FIRST (REAL VOLTA OR FALSE VOLTA? WHY?), THEN TAP EACH CARD TO CHECK
CARD 1 · AFTER EIGHT LINES ABOUT A FRIENDSHIP ENDING…
"But even so, I know our friendship died."
Tap to reveal
ANSWER
False volta. "But even so" promises a reversal, then the line repeats the octave's conclusion. Nothing new is known or seen. Remove the line and the poem reads the same, which is the test that catches it.
CARD 2 · AFTER EIGHT LINES ABOUT A FRIENDSHIP ENDING…
"But grief is proof the friendship was not small."
Tap to reveal
ANSWER
Genuine volta. The speaker reinterprets the octave: the same grief now means something it did not mean before (evidence of worth, not only loss). New knowledge arrives; the argument changes direction while keeping its subject.
➡️ NOW WRITE YOUR OWN (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)

Write two candidate line 9s for your own sonnet: first a deliberately false volta (pivot word, no turn), then a genuine one, where the speaker sees or knows something new. Under the pair, write one sentence naming what changes in the genuine version. Carry the genuine line into your full draft for Project Feeder 3.

💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

A volta is a promise the grammar has to keep: pivot word plus real change. Before you submit your draft, cover lines 9 through 14, read the octave, then uncover line 9 and ask what is new. If you can answer, your turn is earned.
