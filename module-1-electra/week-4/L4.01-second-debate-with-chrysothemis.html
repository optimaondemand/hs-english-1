📖 WHAT YOU ARE READING TODAY
When the right thing is also the dangerous thing, who decides what justice is worth?
Electra's second confrontation with her sister Chrysothemis. Chrysothemis returns to the palace with hope; Electra, believing Orestes dead, answers with a plan. Read for the clash of two positions: act now against stay safe. By the end of the scene, Electra decides to strike alone.

📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

This scene sets two kinds of reasoning side by side, and Sophocles does not let either one win easily. Chrysothemis argues from prudence: she is powerless, the attempt is likely to fail, and a failed revenge helps no one and may cost them their lives. Electra argues from duty: the wrong is real, the debt is real, and to calculate the odds before acting is itself a kind of surrender. What makes the scene great is that Chrysothemis is not written as a coward or a fool. Her caution is genuinely wise, and Sophocles gives her the stronger practical case, so that Electra's choice costs something to admire. When Chrysothemis refuses to join her, Electra does not back down; she resolves to do the deed herself, alone. Notice how the playwright uses the disagreement to reveal character: the same facts produce opposite conclusions, and each sister is defined by which risk she is willing to bear.

TWO SISTERS, ONE SET OF FACTS · ACTION vs. CAUTION
ELECTRA · ACT NOW
reasons from duty
● The wrong is real and the debt is owed.
● To weigh the odds first is to surrender.
● If no one else will act, she will act alone.
vs.
CHRYSOTHEMIS · STAY SAFE
reasons from prudence
● They are powerless; the attempt will likely fail.
● A failed revenge helps no one.
● Survival is worth more than a doomed gesture.
Both sisters look at the same situation. Sophocles gives the cautious side genuine wisdom, so Electra's choice to act is a real choice, not an obvious one.

✅ CHECK YOUR UNDERSTANDING · ANSWER IN YOUR HEAD FIRST, THEN OPEN
▶  1. Say your own answer first: why does Sophocles make Chrysothemis's argument so reasonable?
So that Electra's choice is a real choice, not an obvious one. If caution were clearly foolish, Electra's courage would cost nothing. By giving the cautious side genuine wisdom, the play forces us to weigh whether her act of justice is admirable or reckless.
▶  2. Say your own answer first: what finally pushes Electra to act alone?
Chrysothemis refuses to help, and Electra believes Orestes is dead, so no one else will act. With no ally left, she decides the debt must still be paid, and that she will pay it herself.
💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

The same facts can lead two people to opposite choices. When you argue about whether Electra's revenge is just, remember that the cautious side had real wisdom too. That tension is your essay.
