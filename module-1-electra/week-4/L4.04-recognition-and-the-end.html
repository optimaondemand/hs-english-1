📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

Anagnorisis is the Greek term for a moment of recognition: the point where a character passes from ignorance to knowledge, often discovering who someone truly is. In Greek tragedy this recognition is frequently the turning point that drives the action to its end, and it is exactly what happens here: Orestes reveals himself, Electra's grief flips into joy, and their reunion releases the revenge that follows. Watch how quickly emotion inverts. The urn scene was pure sorrow; recognition turns it inside out into relief, and then that relief is armed and pointed at the killers. When the deed is done, the Chorus announces that the house of Atreus is at last free. But Sophocles leaves the ending strangely abrupt. A curse that answered blood with blood for generations is now answered with more blood, and the play does not pause to say whether the cycle is truly broken or merely continued one more time. That silence is the door into your essay: the play shows you the revenge and its cost, and asks you to judge whether justice was really done.

📚 WORD STUDY — THE VOCABULARY OF TRAGEDY
anagnorisis (an-ag-NOR-i-sis) — the moment a character recognizes a truth or another's real identity; the turn from ignorance to knowledge. Orestes' self-revelation is the play's anagnorisis.
dramatic irony — when the audience knows something a character does not. The urn scene runs on dramatic irony.
catharsis (kuh-THAR-sis) — the release, or cleansing, of strong emotion an audience feels through a tragedy. Recognition and revenge together produce the play's catharsis.
WHERE THE WORDS COME FROM (ETYMOLOGY)
All three are Greek. Anagnorisis joins ana- ("again") with gnosis ("knowing"): literally a re-knowing, the same root that gives us diagnosis and recognize. Catharsis comes from katharos, "pure" or "clean," the root behind cathartic. Irony comes from Greek eiron, a speaker who says less than he knows.

✅ CHECK YOUR UNDERSTANDING · ANSWER IN YOUR HEAD FIRST, THEN OPEN
▶  1. Say your own answer first: what is anagnorisis, and where is it in this scene?
Anagnorisis is a moment of recognition, a character's move from ignorance to knowledge. Here it is the moment Orestes reveals he is alive and Electra recognizes her brother, confirmed by their father's signet ring.
▶  2. Say your own answer first: why might a reader doubt that “freedom is won”?
Because the freedom is won by the same means, killing, that started the curse. The play ends abruptly and does not confirm the cycle of blood is broken, so the closing claim can be read as hope, as truth, or as one more link in the chain.
💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

Recognition turns the whole play on its hinge, and then the play stops before answering its own biggest question. Whether the ending's “freedom” is real is now yours to argue in the essay.
