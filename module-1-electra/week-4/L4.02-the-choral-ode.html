📖 WHAT YOU ARE READING TODAY
How can a single image, a bird that never stops crying, say what plain praise cannot?
The choral ode that follows the sisters' quarrel. Having watched Electra choose her lonely course, the Chorus steps forward to praise her. Read for what the Chorus honors in her, and for the striking image it uses: a bird that never stops crying for the one it has lost.

📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 NO WRONG ANSWER
Think of a sound in nature that carries feeling for you, such as a bird at dawn, wind, or rain. Why can a natural image say something about grief that plain words cannot?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

A choral ode is the Chorus's song between scenes, and its job is to help the audience feel and judge what it has just seen. Here the Chorus responds to Electra's decision by praising her, and it does so through a comparison: she is like a bird that will not cease its cry for what it has lost. The image works on two levels. It captures the sound of her grief, ceaseless and instinctive, and it gives that grief dignity, because the bird's faithfulness is natural and true rather than merely stubborn. The ode then names what the Chorus admires: Electra has chosen to stand with those who suffer for what is right, and so, they say, she wins a double honor of wisdom and piety. Read carefully, though. The ode does not settle the play's hard question. Praising her courage is not the same as proving her revenge just, and Sophocles leaves that gap open on purpose.

THE ODE'S CENTRAL IMAGE · THE UNWEARIED CRY
🐦
♪   ♪   ♪   ♪
“she bewails alway, hapless one,
her father’s fate, like the nightingale…”
endless cry = grief that never rests the faithful bird = wisdom + piety
For the Greeks, the nightingale was the bird whose song is endless mourning. By comparing Electra to it, the Chorus gives her grief a voice that never tires, and gives it honor rather than mere stubbornness.

✅ CHECK YOUR UNDERSTANDING · ANSWER IN YOUR HEAD FIRST, THEN OPEN
▶  1. Say your own answer first: what is the purpose of a choral ode?
It is the Chorus's song between scenes. It voices the community's response and guides the audience in how to feel about and judge what has happened, without acting in the plot itself.
▶  2. Say your own answer first: does the ode prove Electra's revenge is just?
No. It praises her faithfulness, courage, and piety, which is not the same as declaring the coming act of vengeance justified. Sophocles keeps that larger question open.
💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

One image can carry a whole feeling. The nightingale tells you Electra's grief will never rest, and honors it, without ever declaring her revenge just. Praise and proof are not the same thing.
