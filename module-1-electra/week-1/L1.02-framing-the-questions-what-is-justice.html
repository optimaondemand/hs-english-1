📚 PART 1 · WHAT WE MEAN BY JUSTICE
💭 WARM-UP · NO WRONG ANSWER
Someone cuts in line ahead of you. You feel it is unfair. Put your finger on what, exactly, they did wrong.

Justice as giving what is owed

A useful starting point comes from the Greeks themselves: justice is giving what is owed. That one idea explains a surprising range of cases. A thief is unjust because he takes what belongs to another; he owes it back. A person who spreads lies is unjust because we owe one another the truth. Framed this way, justice is really a web of duties, things we owe. But the Greeks saw that these duties can pull in different directions. You may owe one thing to the gods (piety, sacred law), another to the polis, the city organized into a governed state (its laws and its order), and another to your family (loyalty, love, the honoring of your dead). Usually these agree. But what happens when they do not, when honoring your murdered father means defying the ruler of your city, who is also now your family? That is the knot at the center of Electra, and there is no easy way to cut it.

Word origins: polis is hiding in plain sight in English: politics (the affairs of the polis), police, metropolis ("mother-city"). Spotting the root tells you the word's family. Copia (in your Organizer): rewrite the sentence "He broke the rule," twice: once so it names a failure of duty, and once so it names a failure of justice. What shifts between the two?
📚 PART 2 · WHY DUTIES BECOME TRAGEDY

Most of the time your duties agree, and doing right is simply hard, not confusing. Tragedy begins where they cannot all be satisfied at once. Test your working definition against the play's central knot before you read a single line.

✎ CHECK YOURSELF · PICK THE BEST ANSWER
1. What is our working definition of justice?
2. Why might these duties create a tragedy rather than a simple choice?
⚠️ WORTH KNOWING · AI AND DEFINITIONS
An AI tool can give you a dictionary definition of "justice" instantly, and that can be a fine place to check a word. It cannot decide for you which duty should win when two of them collide in Electra. That judgment is the work of this module, and it stays yours.
