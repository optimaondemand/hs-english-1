GRAMMAR 1.01
Subordinate Clauses
Lesson
🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
How can one small word turn two facts into a single, smarter sentence?
You will learn to tell an independent clause from a subordinate one, join them into a complex sentence, punctuate it correctly, and then use the move in your own writing about the play.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 READ BOTH ALOUD · NO WRONG ANSWER
A. Electra mourns. Orestes plots his return.   |   B. While Electra mourns, Orestes plots his return.
Which version shows that the two events happen at the same time?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

An independent clause has a subject and a verb and can stand alone: Orestes plots his return. A subordinate (or dependent) clause also has a subject and a verb, but it cannot stand alone. What makes it subordinate is the word it begins with: a subordinating conjunction such as because, although, when, while, since, if, or after. Add one to "Electra mourns" and it can no longer stand by itself: While Electra mourns… now demands a completion. Joining a subordinate clause to an independent one makes a complex sentence, which lets you show relationships (time, cause, contrast) that a string of short sentences cannot.

ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
Subordinate clause first → use a comma.  Independent clause first → usually no comma.
ON THE BOARD · SPOT THE SUBORDINATE CLAUSE
Although Aegisthus rules the palace, Electra refuses to be silent.
Electra weeps daily because her father’s murder is unavenged.
When the Tutor returns with Orestes, the plan begins.
The gold underline marks the subordinate clause. Notice the comma appears only when that clause comes first.

➡️ NOW WRITE YOUR OWN (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)

Write two complex sentences about the opening of Electra: one that begins with a subordinate clause (remember the comma), and one where the subordinate clause comes second. Use a different subordinating conjunction in each.

💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

A subordinating conjunction turns a stand-alone sentence into a clause that leans on another. Put that clause first, and it earns a comma. You will use complex sentences all module to show cause and contrast in your essay.
