GRAMMAR 2.01
Parallel Structure
Grammar Lesson
🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why does a balanced sentence sound more sure of itself than a lopsided one?
You will recognize when items in a list or a pair should match in grammatical form, fix a sentence whose parts do not match, and use parallel structure on purpose to make your own writing about the play sound balanced and deliberate.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 READ BOTH ALOUD · NO WRONG ANSWER
A. Electra mourns her father, defies her mother, and she is refusing to be silent.   |   B. Electra mourns her father, defies her mother, and refuses to be silent.
Which version sounds smoother and more controlled?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

Parallel structure means that ideas doing the same job in a sentence are written in the same grammatical form. When you list actions, keep all the verbs in the same shape: mourns, defies, refuses, not mourns, defies, is refusing. When you pair or list nouns, keep them all nouns; when you pair phrases, match phrase to phrase. The stumble in version A above comes from mixing a plain verb (defies) with a verb phrase that restarts the subject (she is refusing). Parallel structure matters for two reasons. First, it is simply clearer: matched forms let a reader move through a list without tripping. Second, it is persuasive: balance sounds deliberate, even weighty, which is why speakers reach for it when they want a line to land. Electra's whole quarrel with her sister is built on balanced opposites, such as caution against courage and safety against honor, and you can echo that balance in your own sentences by keeping paired ideas in matching form.

ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
Items in a series → same grammatical form. Match verb to verb, noun to noun, phrase to phrase.
ON THE BOARD · MATCHED ITEMS IN GOLD
Electra mourns, defies, and refuses.
Chrysothemis values safety, comfort, and silence.
The Chorus urges the sisters to think, to speak, and to act with foresight.
The gold items in each line share one form: three verbs, then three nouns, then three infinitives. That matching is what makes the line feel balanced.
✎ YOUR TURN · CHOOSE THE BEST ANSWER
1. Which sentence uses parallel structure correctly?
2. Which part breaks parallel structure? — Electra weeps openly, accuses her mother, and is honoring her dead father.
✎️ IDENTIFY & PRACTICE (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)
Each sentence below breaks parallel structure. In your Organizer, rewrite the part that does not match. Do all five before you open the check.
1. Electra weeps openly, accuses her mother, and is honoring her dead father.
2. The Chorus urges the sisters to think clearly, to speak carefully, and acting with foresight.
3. Chrysothemis chooses comfort, safety, and to survive.
4. Electra tells her sister to reject the gifts, to cut a lock of hair, and she should lay it at the tomb.
5. The dream is frightening, prophetic, and it gives hope.
▶  Write all five fixes in your notebook first, then open to check
1. is honoring → honors (match the plain verbs weeps, accuses, honors).
2. acting with foresight → to act with foresight (match the infinitives).
3. to survive → survival (match the nouns comfort, safety, survival).
4. she should lay it at the tomb → to lay it at the tomb (match the infinitives).
5. it gives hope → hopeful (match the adjectives frightening, prophetic, hopeful).
➡️ NOW WRITE YOUR OWN (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)

Write two sentences about this week's reading that use parallel structure on purpose. Make one a list of three matched verbs (what Electra does), and make one a balanced pair that contrasts the sisters (for example, "Chrysothemis chooses ___, while Electra chooses ___," with matching forms on both sides).

💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

Match verb to verb, noun to noun, phrase to phrase. Balanced sentences sound sure of themselves, which is exactly the tone you want when you argue about justice in your module essay.
