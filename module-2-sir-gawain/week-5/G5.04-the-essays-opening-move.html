GRAMMAR 5.04
The Essay’s Opening Move
Lesson
🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
How do you turn a beautiful, Pearl-Poet-style catalog paragraph into the opening of a real argument?
Your essay opens with an alliterative catalog paragraph, the same imitation move you have practiced all module. Today you learn to make that paragraph do work: it must set a scene, then pivot to your disputed question and land on your thesis. You will study a worked opening and revise your own against clear criteria.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 NO WRONG ANSWER
A movie opens with a slow, gorgeous shot of a castle. What does that opening owe the rest of the film?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

A strong opening paragraph makes three moves in order. First, the catalog: a vivid, alliterative list in the Pearl Poet’s style that paints the scene or the knight. Second, the pivot: a transition sentence that turns from the picture to the problem, naming the disputed question. Third, the thesis: your one-sentence, direct answer to that question. The catalog earns the reader; the pivot points them; the thesis commits you.

THE OPENING, IN THREE MOVES
Catalog (paint it) → Pivot (name the question) → Thesis (answer it).
ON THE BOARD · A WORKED OPENING PARAGRAPH
Green as glass and grim as winter, the strange knight strides into Camelot, bearing a bright axe and a bold bargain. The court that feasts in gold will, by the tale’s end, wear a girdle of shame turned to honor. Between that entrance and that ending lies a question the poem never settles: is Sir Gawain a man of honor? He is: the pentangle’s five virtues survive a single, forgivable lapse, and the poem’s own mercy proves it.
■ gold = the alliterative catalog  ·  ■ light blue = the pivot toward the question  ·  ■ rose = the disputed question  ·  ■ white (bold) = the thesis
Notice the alliteration in the catalog (“green as glass and grim,” “bright axe and a bold bargain”) and how the paragraph narrows, sentence by sentence, from a picture to a single claim.
✎ YOUR TURN · CHOOSE THE BEST ANSWER
1. In the worked paragraph, which move is the pivot?
2. Which sentence works as a thesis?
➡️ NOW REVISE YOUR OWN OPENING (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)

Take the opening paragraph you drafted in Week 4 and revise it against these criteria. Check each box when your paragraph meets it.

☐ The catalog is at least three items, vivid, with at least two alliterative pairs.
☐ A clear pivot sentence turns from the image to the disputed question.
☐ The paragraph names the question: Is Sir Gawain a man of honor?
☐ It ends on a one-sentence thesis that takes a side someone could dispute.
☐ No em dashes; the paragraph narrows from picture to point.
💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

A great opening is not just beautiful; it is beautiful and pointed. Let the catalog draw the reader in, then make the pivot, then commit to your thesis. Beauty that leads somewhere is the Pearl Poet’s own trick.
