GRAMMAR 5.05
Periodic and Cumulative Sentences
Lesson
🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
Does your sentence make the reader wait for the point, or hand it over first and pile on the detail?
Two sentence shapes give your essay rhythm and control. A periodic sentence saves its main point for the end, building suspense. A cumulative sentence states its point first, then adds detail after. You met the periodic sentence in Week 1; today you learn its opposite and when to use each.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 READ BOTH ALOUD · NO WRONG ANSWER
A. Through winter woods, past cold rivers, alone and afraid, Gawain rode.   |   B. Gawain rode through winter woods, past cold rivers, alone and afraid.
Which one makes you wait longest to learn who is doing the riding?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

A periodic sentence withholds its main clause until the end. Everything before it is build-up, so the reader leans forward, waiting for the point to land. It creates suspense and emphasis, which is why the Pearl Poet uses it to stage the Green Knight’s entrance. A cumulative sentence (also called a loose sentence) does the reverse: it opens with the main clause, then keeps adding phrases that enrich it. It feels natural and flowing, and it is useful when you want to state a claim and then unfold the details behind it.

THE ONE-LINE TEST
Where is the main point? At the end → periodic (suspense).  At the start → cumulative (flow).
ON THE BOARD · TWO SENTENCE SHAPES, SAME IDEA
PERIODIC · MAIN CLAUSE LAST
Though he wore the pentangle, though he kept the pact for two days, though he feared no blade, Gawain hid the girdle.
CUMULATIVE · MAIN CLAUSE FIRST
Gawain hid the girdle, breaking the pact, betraying his host, trading a knight’s honor for one more day of life.
Same core clause, opposite placement. The periodic version makes you wait for “Gawain hid the girdle”; the cumulative version leads with it, then piles on the cost.
✎ YOUR TURN · CHOOSE THE BEST ANSWER
1. Which sentence is periodic?
2. Which sentence is cumulative?
3. You want to build suspense before revealing Gawain’s choice. Which shape fits?
➡️ NOW WRITE YOUR OWN (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)

Find one sentence in your essay draft. Rewrite it twice: once as a periodic sentence (main clause held to the end) and once as a cumulative sentence (main clause first, details after). Then decide which version serves that spot in your essay better, and keep it.

💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

Sentence shape is a choice, not an accident. Save the point for the end when you want suspense; lead with it when you want flow. A page of only one shape gets dull; alternate them and your essay finds a rhythm.
