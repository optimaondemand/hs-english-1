🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
How can a question be more powerful than a statement, even when nobody answers it?
You will learn what a rhetorical question is, how it makes its point, and how to use one to open or sharpen an argument, including the disputatio essay you begin drafting this week.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 READ BOTH ALOUD · NO WRONG ANSWER
A. You should not have hidden the girdle.   |   B. How could a true knight hide the girdle and call himself faithful?
Which version makes you feel the accusation more sharply, and why?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

A rhetorical question is a question asked for effect rather than to get an answer. The speaker already knows the answer, and expects you to know it too. Because you supply the answer yourself, the point feels like your own conclusion, which makes it land harder than a flat statement. Writers use rhetorical questions to express strong feeling, to challenge a reader, or to raise the very question an argument will then answer. Gawain, cursing himself at the Green Chapel, reaches for questions like these, because a plain sentence cannot carry that much shame.

ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
A rhetorical question ends with a question mark, but its purpose is to make a point, not to gather information. If the honest answer is obvious to everyone, it is rhetorical.
ON THE BOARD · STATEMENT → RHETORICAL QUESTION
Plain: Fear does not excuse a broken promise.
Rhetorical: Since when does fear excuse a broken promise?
Plain: No knight who hid the girdle can call himself faithful.
Rhetorical: How can a knight who hid the girdle call himself faithful?
Plain: The court's laughter may be kindness or evasion.
Rhetorical: Is the court's laughter mercy, or a refusal to look?
The gold label marks each rhetorical version. Notice that the third one genuinely opens a debate: a rhetorical question can also frame the exact quaestio your essay will argue.
✎ USE WITH CARE
One well-placed rhetorical question is powerful. A paragraph full of them turns pushy and thin. In an essay, use a rhetorical question to open a point or turn it, then answer it with real evidence and reasoning.

➡️ NOW WRITE YOUR OWN (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)

Write three rhetorical questions you could use in your disputatio essay about Gawain's honor. Make at least one that frames the debate (like the third board example) and at least one that presses an objection. Beside each, jot the answer you expect the reader to supply.

💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

A rhetorical question makes the reader answer in their own head, so the point feels earned rather than told. Use it to open or turn an argument, then deliver the evidence. You will use at least one in this week's essay opening.
