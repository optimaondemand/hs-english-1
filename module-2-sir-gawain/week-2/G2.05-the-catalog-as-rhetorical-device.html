The Catalog as a Rhetorical Device

🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
Why does the poet name beast after beast, cold after cold, instead of just saying "the journey was hard"?
You will learn what a catalog is, why writers use one, how the Pearl Poet stacks Gawain's hardships to overwhelm the reader, and how to build a controlled catalog of your own.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 READ BOTH ALOUD · NO WRONG ANSWER
A. The trip was exhausting.   |   B. There were the delays, the lost bag, the missed train, the rain, the aching feet, the cold coffee.
Which one actually makes you feel how exhausting the trip was?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

A catalog is a deliberate list: a series of items, images, or actions set side by side to build up a single strong impression. Poets and speakers have used it for thousands of years. Its power is cumulative. One hardship is a fact; a dozen hardships in a row become an experience. In the winter journey of Fitt II, the poet catalogs Gawain's trials: sleet, bare rock, hunger, dragons and wolves, wild men, bulls and bears and boars, and giants. He does not have to say "the journey was terrible." The list is the terror, and Gawain's survival of the whole list is the proof of his endurance.

WHAT MAKES A CATALOG WORK
Choose items that build the same impression; keep them in parallel form; and order them so the last item lands hardest.
ON THE BOARD · THE CATALOG OF HARDSHIPS (FITT II)
He suffered…
● the sleet that near slew him on the bare rocks
● cold nights slept in his harness
● dragons and wolves
● wild men of the woods
● bulls, and bears, and wild boars
● giants… and still he rode on.
Six blows of hardship, each in the same parallel shape, piling up so the reader feels the journey's weight. The turn at the end, "and still he rode on," is where the catalog proves Gawain's virtue.

➡️ NOW WRITE YOUR OWN (IN YOUR ORGANIZER)

Write a six-item catalog that builds one strong impression of a person, place, or hardship. Keep every item in parallel form, and save your hardest-hitting item for last. This is direct practice for your Quick Write 2 virtue catalog, so choose a subject you might use there.

💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

A catalog turns telling into experiencing. Stack parallel items toward one impression, land the strongest last, and your reader feels the weight instead of just hearing about it.
