Of all the tales he could tell, why does the Knight tell a grand story of noble love and honorable war?

📚 PART 1 · THE STORY THE KNIGHT CHOOSES
💭 WARM-UP · NO WRONG ANSWER
Suppose you had to tell a story to a group of strangers and you wanted them to respect you. What kind of story would you pick?

Who tells first, and why it matters

On the road to Canterbury the pilgrims agree to a story contest, and they draw lots to decide who begins. The lot falls to the Knight, the highest-ranking traveler in the company. He is a seasoned soldier who has fought in holy wars, and he embodies chivalry: the medieval code that a knight should be brave, loyal, honorable, courteous, and devoted to a noble cause. What he tells is not a report from his own battles. It is a polished tale of ancient Athens, and its polish is the point. The kind of story a person chooses tells you what that person honors.

📚 PART 2 · THREE WORDS FOR A NOBLE STORY

Chivalry, romance, and the shape of the tale

A romance, in the literary sense, is a genre: a long adventure story of noble heroes, high ideals, love, and honor, usually set in a world grander than everyday life. The Knight's Tale is a model romance. Two cousins of Thebes, Palamon and Arcite, are captured in battle by Duke Theseus and locked in a tower. From that tower both glimpse the princess Emelye walking in a garden, and both fall instantly in love. Their friendship breaks into rivalry, and the conflict is finally settled by a formal tournament staged with kings, temples, and prayers to the gods. Every element (the noble birth, the sworn love, the code of honor, the ceremony) is the machinery of chivalric romance.

ANATOMY OF A CHIVALRIC ROMANCE · MAPPED ONTO THE KNIGHT'S TALE
Noble heroes
Palamon and Arcite, princes of Thebes
Sworn, idealized love
Both worship Emelye from afar
A code of honor
The rivalry is fought by rules, not murder
Ceremony and the gods
A grand tournament; prayers to Venus, Mars, Diana
An orderly world
Theseus imposes order on chaos and grief
Read down the list: this is a story about how a noble society keeps order even when love turns friends into rivals. That is what the Knight admires.

Starting in the middle: in medias res

In medias res is a Latin phrase meaning "into the middle of things." It names an old epic technique: instead of beginning at the true start of the story, the poet drops you into the action already in motion and fills in the past later. The Knight's Tale opens this way. We do not begin with Palamon and Arcite as children. We arrive as Duke Theseus is already riding home from conquering an entire kingdom, and almost at once a crowd of grieving widows throws itself in his path begging for justice. The war that captures our two heroes is launched before we have caught our breath. Beginning in the thick of great events is itself a signal that this is meant to feel like epic.

📚 WORD STUDY
chivalry: the medieval code of honor for knights (courage, loyalty, courtesy, service).  ·  romance (genre): a long adventure story of noble heroes, high love, and honor.  ·  in medias res: beginning a story "in the middle of things," with the action already moving.
Word origins: chivalry comes from Old French chevalerie, from cheval (horse): a knight was, first of all, a man on horseback. Romance comes from Old French romanz, meaning a work written in the everyday Romance language rather than Latin; over time it named the adventurous tales told in that language. In medias res is Latin and comes to us from the Roman poet Horace, who used it to describe how epic should begin.
Copia (in your Organizer): take the plain sentence "The knight told a story," and rewrite it three times: once so the word chivalry reveals his values, once so the word romance names his genre, and once so the phrase in medias res describes how he opens. Expand each with detail from the tale.
