GRAMMAR 4.04
Periodic vs. Cumulative Sentences
Lesson
🎯 TODAY'S FOCUS
Where you put the main point of a sentence changes what the reader feels: suspense, or momentum.
You met the periodic sentence in Week 1. Today you set it beside its opposite, the cumulative sentence, and learn to choose between them on purpose. This is the exact skill Quick Write 4 tests: the Wife's voice and your analytical voice should not be built from the same sentence shapes.
📚 PART 1 · WARM-UP
💭 READ BOTH ALOUD · NO WRONG ANSWER
A. After a year of searching, after every answer had failed him, on the last day of his life, the knight found her.   |   B. The knight found her at last, worn from a year of searching, empty of answers, one day from death.
Which version makes you wait longer to learn what happened?
📚 PART 2 · THE LESSON

A periodic sentence saves its main clause for the end. Modifiers, conditions, and buildup come first; the point lands last. The effect is suspense and emphasis: the reader must hold everything in mind until the final words snap it into place. It sounds formal, deliberate, composed. That is why it suits an analytical voice delivering a judgment.

A cumulative sentence (also called a loose sentence) does the reverse. It opens with the main clause, then piles on detail: phrases and clauses that extend, sharpen, and color the point already made. The effect is momentum and voice: it moves like living speech, one thing tumbling after another. That is why it suits a talker like the Wife of Bath, who never says one thing where five will do.

THE ONE RULE TO REMEMBER
Periodic = buildup first, main clause last (suspense).  Cumulative = main clause first, detail after (momentum).
ON THE BOARD · FIND THE MAIN CLAUSE
Periodic: Though she quotes scripture, though she counts her husbands like trophies, though she laughs at her own confessions, the Wife is making an argument.

Cumulative: The Wife is making an argument, quoting scripture when it suits her, counting her husbands like trophies, laughing at confessions that would shame another speaker.
Same facts in both. The gold underline marks the main clause: last in the periodic sentence, first in the cumulative one. Feel how the first delays and the second accelerates.

💪 THE ONE THING TO CARRY FORWARD

Sentence shape is voice. Cumulative sentences talk; periodic sentences pronounce. In Quick Write 4 you will build one paragraph that talks like the Wife and one that pronounces like a critic, and the reader should hear the difference in the very first sentence of each.
